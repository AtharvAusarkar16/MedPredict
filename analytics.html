Key Insights

Stay Duration Trends

Patients with higher severity scores (2-3) typically have longer hospital stays, with an average increase of 2-3 days compared to mild cases. Emergency admissions also show slightly longer stays than planned admissions.

Demographic Patterns

The dataset shows a relatively even age distribution with peaks in middle-aged patients (40-60 years). Older patients tend to have longer stays, correlating with increased complexity of care needs.

Model Performance

Our Random Forest model uses 15 engineered features with no multicollinearity. The model achieves an R² score of 0.1023 with a Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of 14.46 days, providing data-driven insights for hospital stay prediction.

Financial Correlation

Higher deposit amounts show a slight inverse correlation with stay duration, potentially indicating better pre-planning or less complex cases among patients with higher financial resources.
