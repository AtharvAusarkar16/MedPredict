Key Insights & Recommendations

Stay Duration Patterns

Analysis of real hospital data shows that patients with higher severity scores (2-3) have significantly longer stays, with an average increase of 15-25 days compared to mild cases. Emergency admissions also correlate with extended stays.

Demographic Insights

The dataset reveals interesting age patterns with peaks in middle-aged patients (40-60 years). Older patients (60+) show longer average stays, suggesting increased complexity of care requirements with age.

Financial Correlations

Deposit amounts show a moderate inverse correlation with stay duration, potentially indicating that patients with higher financial resources may have shorter planned stays or better pre-arranged care.

Social Factors

Visitor frequency data suggests that patients with more visitors tend to have slightly shorter stays, possibly due to better support systems or motivation for quicker recovery.
